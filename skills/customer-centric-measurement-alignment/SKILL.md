# Customer-Centric Measurement Alignment

## When to Use
Apply this skill when:
- The business is service-oriented OR decentralized
- Current operational metrics do not reflect customer promises
- Employees in non-customer-facing roles lack clear feedback on customer impact
- Frontline personnel cannot determine if their decisions accomplish overall company goals

Do NOT use if current metrics already accurately reflect customer needs.

## Prerequisites
- Identified customer promises (what value the business guarantees)
- Existing operational metrics (KPIs currently being tracked)

## Variables
- **Customer Promise**: The specific value the business guarantees to the customer (e.g., delivery time, precision)
- **Current Metric**: The KPI currently being tracked (e.g., volume, paperwork separation)

## Execution Steps

### 1. Identify the Basic Mistake
Avoid the trap of promising one thing while measuring another:
- Recognize mismatches between promises and metrics
- Example: Promising "prompt and precise cargo delivery" while measuring "volume and paperwork integrity"
- Understand that a shipment can arrive days late without being recorded as "delayed" if only volume/paperwork is measured

### 2. Realign Goals to Customer Needs
Shift focus from internal executive goals to what customers value:
- Define success from the customer's perspective
- Translate customer promises into measurable outcomes
- Example: Redefine "precision" as "prompt deliveries to specified locations" rather than internal efficiency metrics

### 3. Establish Feedback for Non-Customer-Facing Roles
Create clear targets for employees who affect customer service without direct contact:
- Identify roles with indirect customer impact (e.g., baggage handlers, cargo loaders)
- Define concrete success criteria that connect their work to customer outcomes
- Example: "Luggage on the belt when passengers arrive" as the success metric for baggage handlers

### 4. Adapt for Decentralized Structures
Ensure frontline personnel have accurate feedback systems:
- Provide direct feedback mechanisms since top-down filtering of standards is unreliable
- Enable frontline decision-makers to verify if their actions accomplish company goals
- Support autonomous judgment with measurable success indicators

## Expected Output
A realigned measurement strategy where metrics directly track the fulfillment of customer promises rather than internal volume or administrative efficiency.
