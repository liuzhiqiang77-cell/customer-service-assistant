# Service Investment Implementation

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Strategic goals target specific customer segments (e.g., business travelers)
- Current service offerings are inadequate to meet these strategic goals
- Cost reduction strategies have been exhausted
- You need to implement a comprehensive service transformation
- Multiple coordinated improvement projects are required

## Prerequisites

Before executing this skill, ensure:
- [ ] Board approval for significant capital investment
- [ ] Clearly defined strategic goal
- [ ] Stakeholder willingness to accept investment risk
- [ ] Execution capability to manage multiple projects

## Execution Steps

### 1. Secure Capital Investment Approval
- Obtain approval for capital injection
- Document investment rationale and expected outcomes
- Establish governance structure for fund allocation

### 2. Approve Operating Budget Increase
- Secure approval for annual operating expense increase
- Align budget increases with project timelines
- Establish monitoring mechanisms for ongoing expenses

### 3. Allocate Funds to Projects
- Identify specific projects aligned with strategic goals
- Distribute capital and operating funds across projects
- Prioritize projects based on impact and feasibility

### 4. Execute Key Projects
Implement critical initiatives such as:
- **Punctuality Campaign**: Launch comprehensive on-time performance improvement
- **Infrastructure Enhancement**: Improve traffic hubs and key facilities
- **Staff Training**: Provide service courses for frontline and support staff
- **Service Detail Restoration**: Reintroduce premium service elements

### 5. Accept and Manage Risk
- Acknowledge that revenue increase is not guaranteed
- Monitor performance metrics closely
- Be prepared to adjust strategy based on results
- Document lessons learned for future investments

## Risk Considerations

- **Revenue Risk**: No guarantee that increased expenses will generate proportional revenue
- **Execution Risk**: Requires strong project management capabilities
- **Strategic Risk**: Investment may not achieve desired market positioning
- **Financial Risk**: Significant capital and operating commitments

## Expected Outcomes

- Execution of multiple service improvement projects
- Enhanced staff capabilities through training
- Improved service quality and customer experience
- Better alignment with strategic customer segments
