# Turnaround Strategy Case Studies

## Linjeflyg
- **Situation:** Financial difficulties in regional airline
- **Strategy:** Market reorientation, frontline empowerment
- **Result:** Successful turnaround with employee energy

## Vingresor
- **Situation:** Travel company facing market challenges
- **Strategy:** Vision alignment, responsibility redistribution
- **Result:** Execution of new plans with high employee engagement

## SAS (Scandinavian Airlines)
- **Situation:** Deep financial crisis, bureaucratic culture
- **Strategy:** Complete organizational transformation, moments of truth focus
- **Result:** Market leadership, industry benchmark for service

## SJ (Swedish Railway)
- **Situation:** State railway needing competitive positioning
- **Strategy:** Provocative competition against airlines
- **Result:** Energized organization, clear market position

## Key Learnings Across Cases

1. **No identical programs:** Each turnaround required distinct adaptation to specific market needs
2. **Employee willingness critical:** Success required employees willing to devote extra time and effort
3. **Leadership shift mandatory:** Transformation failed when leaders remained "managers" rather than becoming "leaders"
4. **Vision essential:** Clear picture of what company could become guided all decisions
5. **Risky tactics sometimes necessary:** Leaking plans, provoking competitors created needed energy

## Contrasting Examples

### Bob Crandall (American Airlines)
- **Approach:** Started with customer needs and market conditions
- **Result:** Successful strategy aligned with market

### Production-Oriented Airline Executive
- **Approach:** Started with internal goals, then examined market
- **Result:** Strategy disconnected from customer needs, failure
