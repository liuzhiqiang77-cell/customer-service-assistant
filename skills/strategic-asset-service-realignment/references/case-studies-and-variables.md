# Strategic Asset & Service Realignment - Reference Details

## Case Study: SAS Turnaround

### Background
- Losses: $30 million in 1979 and 1980
- Investment: $50 million for 147 service improvement projects
- Result: Return to profitability in one year, named "Airline of the Year 1984"

### Implementation Details

#### Target Audience
- **Primary Segment:** Frequent business traveler
- **Rationale:** High-value customers with urgent scheduling needs

#### Service Product: EuroClass
- **Concept:** First-class service at coach rates
- **Differentiation:** Specialized offerings for business travelers

#### Fleet Strategy
- **Mothballed:** Airbuses and flagship 747s (large, efficient but inflexible)
- **Retained:** DC-9s (older, less fuel-efficient but more flexible)
- **Reasoning:** Business travelers need frequency and schedule flexibility

#### Service Investment
- **Total Investment:** $50 million
- **Number of Projects:** 147 service improvement projects
- **Timing:** Executed while company was losing money
- **Focus:** Direct impact on business traveler experience

## Critical Success Factors

1. **Willingness to Challenge Conventional Wisdom**
   - Ignore traditional efficiency metrics
   - Prioritize service metrics over cost metrics

2. **Customer-Centric Decision Making**
   - Every asset decision must serve the target segment
   - Flexibility trumps efficiency

3. **Bold Investment During Crisis**
   - Invest in service even when losing money
   - Focus on long-term customer relationships

4. **Clear Segment Focus**
   - Don't try to serve all segments
   - Concentrate resources on the most valuable segment

## Applicable Industries

- **Airlines:** Fleet reallocation, service class redesign
- **Turnaround Situations:** Any industry with physical assets facing crisis
- **Service Companies:** Hotels, restaurants, transportation, hospitality

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Over-optimizing for efficiency** at the expense of customer service
2. **Trying to serve all segments** instead of focusing on the critical one
3. **Hesitating to invest** during financial losses
4. **Keeping large, inflexible assets** because they look good on paper
