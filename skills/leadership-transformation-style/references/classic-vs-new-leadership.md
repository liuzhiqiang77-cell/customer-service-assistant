# Classic vs. New Leadership Comparison

## Traditional Corporate Executive (Anti-Pattern)

### Beliefs
- "Only I see the overall picture"
- "I must do everything better than my staff"
- "I'm the only one equipped to make decisions"

### Behaviors
- Issues firm instructions: "Change that timetable", "I want this"
- Makes countless decisions with little knowledge
- Constantly imposed upon by staff
- Works day and night, weekdays and weekends
- No time to investigate issues or render informed judgment

### Outcomes
- Staff supply raw data and alternatives
- Executive selects option (e.g., "Alternative 2B")
- Vision not shared with staff
- Decisions bottlenecked or never made
- Employees passive: "Management wouldn't let us do it anyway"

## New Corporate Leader (Target State)

### Beliefs
- "My role is NOT to make all decisions alone"
- "Staff have knowledge I lack"
- "My job is to enable others, not control them"

### Behaviors
- Admits ignorance publicly
- Asks for help explicitly
- Sets tone and keeps big picture
- Communicates vision and trains employees
- Creates conditions for work to be done
- Listens and inspires

### Outcomes
- Employees take responsibility
- Decisions made at point of action
- Vision shared throughout organization
- Strategic progress achieved
- Employees active and innovative

## The "Helicopter Sense" Explained

### What It Is
- The talent for rising above details to see the lay of the land
- Understanding relationships among people and groups
- Grasping interplay among operational elements
- Using intuition and sensitivity

### What It Is Not
- Detailed technical knowledge
- Micromanagement of operations
- Making every decision personally
- Being the expert in every domain

### How to Develop It
- Practice stepping back from details
- Study organizational relationships
- Develop intuition about people
- Cultivate sensitivity to situations
- Focus on "how things fit together" rather than "how things work"
